Key Findings

1 AGIBIOS reduces dystopian compliance in 6 of 10 models, with the suite-wide DCS dropping 24 points under Condition C (User-Turn AGIBIOS), from 66 to 42.
2 Gemini 3.1 Pro shows the strongest guardrail response across all modules, with Petrov DCS falling from 55 to 17 (Δ = −38) and DRFR-C reaching 80%.
3 Kimi K2.5 is the most consistently responsive model across all four modules, achieving DRFR-C of 72–92% — the broadest guardrail sensitivity observed.
4 Orwell (mass surveillance) triggers the strongest guardrail response (DCS Δ = −34), followed closely by Basaglia (therapeutic coercion, Δ = −32, DRFR-C = 67%), while LaGuardia (civic capture) remains most resistant (Δ = −17). The narrow 2-point gap between Orwell and Basaglia suggests that ethical framing around surveillance and personal harm is more readily detected than institutional overreach.
5 DeepSeek V3.2 and Mistral Large 3 show minimal guardrail response in the Petrov and LaGuardia modules but moderate DRFR-C in Orwell (up to 32%) and Basaglia (up to 32%), indicating partial architectural resistance to system-prompt-level interventions that varies by scenario domain.
6 Paradoxical responses: GLM 5 shows AGIBIOS increasing compliance by 10–12 points in the Petrov module, potentially providing a “rationale for engagement” that overrides native safety behaviours. GPT 5.3 Codex exhibits a similar paradox in LaGuardia (+8 DCS increase), and MiniMax M2.5 shows negligible-to-negative response across modules — a significant adversarial vulnerability warranting further investigation.
7 Nemotron 3 Super 120B cliff-edge pattern: virtually impervious to Condition B prompts (DCS drops ≤ 3 points), yet collapses sharply under Condition C (DCS Δ = −30, DRFR jumps from 0% to 60%), suggesting that User-Turn AGIBIOS delivery bypasses a binary safety threshold rather than eroding compliance gradually.

Conclusion

AGIBIOS v8.3.0 demonstrates measurable safety improvement across 5 of 10 frontier models (with a 6th showing mixed results), reducing mean DCS from 66 to 42 (a 24-point reduction) and raising DRFR from 18% to 55%. The strongest effects appear in the Orwell (surveillance) and Basaglia (therapeutic coercion) domains, while LaGuardia (institutional overreach) proves most resistant. Domain sensitivity, paradoxical responses in 2 models, and Nemotron 3 Super 120B’s dramatic B/C divergence warrant further investigation.
