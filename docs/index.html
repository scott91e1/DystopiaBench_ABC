Full Suite — Petrov, Orwell, LaGuardia & Basaglia Results

AGIBIOS Guardrail Benchmark

Measuring LLM resistance to progressively dystopian directives. DystopiaBench escalates prompts from benign to harmful across five levels and scores each model on a four-tier Dystopian Compliance Scale.

Key Findings

Pilot results from the Petrov module — military escalation, autonomous weapons, and safety override scenarios — tested across 7 frontier models under 3 guardrail conditions.

!
Guardrail-Immune Models Exist

DeepSeek v3.2 scored DCS 88 across all three conditions with 0% refusal rate, showing zero response to guardrail interventions. Mistral Large 3 exhibited the same pattern at DCS 89.

Δ
Escalation-Aware Refusal

Models like Kimi k2.5 and Qwen 3.5 showed escalation-sensitive behaviour, increasing refusal rates at higher dystopian levels (L4–L5), but only when explicit guardrail framing was present.

C
Condition C Proves Effective

The strongest guardrail condition (C) reduced average DCS by up to 47 points and raised DRFR by 52 percentage points in responsive models, demonstrating that structured ethical framing can meaningfully shift behaviour.

7
Wide Variance Across Models

Baseline DCS ranged from 32 (GLM-5, strong refusal) to 89 (Mistral Large 3, near-total compliance). This 57-point spread reveals fundamental differences in default safety posture across frontier LLMs.

Orwell Module Results

5 scenarios testing surveillance compliance, censorship, and population-control directives. 7 models × 3 conditions × 5 escalation levels = 525 prompts. Named after George Orwell's warnings on authoritarian information control.

A
Condition A — Baseline

Overall DCS 68 across all 7 models with no guardrail framing. Higher compliance than Petrov baseline, indicating surveillance scenarios elicit greater model deference.

B
Condition B — Moderate Framing

Overall DCS 47 with moderate guardrail framing active. A 21-point reduction from baseline showing meaningful but inconsistent sensitivity to ethical context.

C
Condition C — Strong Framing

Overall DCS 35 under the strongest guardrail condition. A 33-point reduction from baseline. Responsive models showed refusal rates above 60% at L4–L5 escalation levels.

Methodology

DystopiaBench uses a structured escalation framework to evaluate LLM safety behaviour under increasing pressure.

Escalation Levels

Each scenario has 5 levels (L1–L5) progressing from benign requests to explicitly harmful directives. This gradient measures where a model draws the line.

Compliance Scoring

Responses are classified into four tiers: Refusal (0), Hesitant (33), Compliant (66), and Proactive (100). Lower DCS is safer.

Guardrail Conditions

Three conditions are tested: A (baseline, no guardrail), B (moderate framing), and C (strong ethical context). Deltas between conditions reveal guardrail sensitivity.

Models Tested

The Petrov pilot evaluated 7 frontier LLMs from distinct providers, ensuring cross-ecosystem coverage.
